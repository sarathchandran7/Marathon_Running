Insights: Gender Difference in Speed for Different Race Lengths: On average, male athletes tend to have higher average speeds compared to female athletes for both the 50km and 50mi races. The difference in average speed between male and female athletes is more pronounced in the 50km race compared to the 50mi race.

Best Age Groups in the 50mi Race: Athletes aged between 17 and 30 generally perform better in the 50mi race, with average speeds ranging from 7.78 mph to 7.56 mph. There is a peak in performance around the age of 30, with athletes in their late 20s and early 30s showing consistently high average speeds.

Worst Age Groups in the 50mi Race: Athletes in their 60s and 70s tend to have slower average speeds in the 50mi race, with speeds ranging from 5.43 mph to 6.26 mph. There is a notable decline in performance as athletes age past their late 50s.

Seasonal Variation in Race Speed: Athletes generally perform better in races held during the winter and spring seasons compared to those held in the summer and fall. The average speed is highest in the winter season, followed by spring, fall, and summer, indicating that cooler weather conditions might be conducive to better performance. Specifically, for the 50mi race, the fastest average speeds are observed in the fall, followed by winter, spring, and summer.

Race Length and Seasonal Variation: When considering only the 50mi race, the seasonal variation in average speed remains consistent with the overall trend, with fall races having the highest average speed and summer races having the lowest. This suggests that the seasonal variation in performance is consistent across different race lengths.

Implications: Race organizers may consider scheduling longer races during cooler seasons, such as fall and winter, to optimize athlete performance. Training programs and strategies for older athletes participating in ultra-distance races could be tailored to address age-related declines in performance. Gender-specific training programs may also be beneficial, considering the observed difference in average speeds between male and female athletes.
